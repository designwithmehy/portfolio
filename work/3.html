Mobile Web Redesign

Healthcare Platform

Mobile Web

Patient engagement on the mobile web was critically low — only 12% of users completed their care tasks. The existing interface was a desktop-first design crammed into a mobile viewport with poor accessibility.

Role

Lead Product Designer

Team

2 designers, 5 engineers, 1 PM

Scope

Mobile web app, Fintech, Accessibility

Duration

5 months

The Problem

A desktop experience forced onto mobile.

Patient engagement on the mobile web was critically low — only 12% of users completed their care tasks. The existing interface was a desktop-first design crammed into a mobile viewport with poor accessibility, making it nearly impossible for patients to manage their health digitally.

12% Completion Rate

Critically low engagement — most patients abandoned care tasks before finishing.

Accessibility Failures

No WCAG compliance — small touch targets and poor contrast excluded key patient groups.

Desktop-First Design

Interface built for desktop, scaled down — not designed for mobile-first needs.

3.2s Load Time

Slow performance on low-bandwidth connections increased abandonment rates further.

The Solution

Task-based architecture, accessibility-first.

1

Accessibility Audit

Conducted a comprehensive accessibility audit and usability testing with 25 patients — including users with visual and motor impairments — to identify every failure point.

2

Task-Based Architecture

Redesigned the entire mobile web experience around a task-based model — patients see their care tasks clearly and can complete them in minimal steps, with large touch targets throughout.

3

Simplified Care Timeline

Built a simplified care timeline that surfaces the most relevant tasks first, with progressive disclosure for complexity — reducing cognitive load while keeping all information accessible.

4

Progressive Enhancement

Built with progressive enhancement for low-bandwidth scenarios — core functionality works even on slow connections, with performance improvements that took load time from 3.2s to 1.1s.

Outcome

From 12% to 89% task completion.

The redesign didn't just improve metrics — it fundamentally changed how patients interact with their healthcare digitally, while achieving full WCAG AA compliance.
