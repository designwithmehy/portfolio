Efficiency Redesign

Fund Transfer

Workflow Redesign

Transformed a multi-day manual transfer process into a guided workflow completed in minutes — eliminating external spreadsheet dependency and reducing error risk across high-value client accounts.

Role

Senior Product Designer

Users

Financial Advisors, Client Services

Scope

User Research, Digital Submission Flows, Paper-to-Digital, Usability Testing

Outcome

30% Time Reduction

The Problem

A fragmented process burdened by manual effort.

High Time Cost

Multi-day transfers driven by disconnected steps, external spreadsheets, and manual calculations.

Manual Risk

Error-prone Excel calculations outside the system.

Repetitive Entry

Redundant transaction data entry across steps.

Inconsistency

Personal workarounds led to execution gaps.

Low Learnability

Steep learning curve for new advisors.

Client Risk

Potential dissatisfaction due to delays or errors.

My Approach

Research first, always.

1

Contextual Inquiry

Shadowed 4 financial advisors through complete end-to-end transfer workflows — not the documented process, but what advisors actually did, including their spreadsheet workarounds.

2

As-Is Journey Mapping

Mapped the full workflow: 10 discrete steps, 5 handoff points, 3 external tools. Found that 60%+ of errors occurred specifically during the manual allocation phase.

3

Co-Design Workshops

Ran collaborative sessions with power users and compliance leads to validate the consolidated flow before committing to high-fidelity design — reducing costly late-stage pivots.

Prototype

Interactive Prototype

The redesigned flow collapses 10 manual steps into a single guided event — with inline allocation, automated transaction preview, and real-time validation before anything is committed.

Outcome

An operational transformation,
not just a redesign.

What took days now takes minutes. That's not a UX improvement — it's an operational transformation that directly reduced client risk and contributed to a major retention win.
