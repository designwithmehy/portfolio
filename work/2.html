Money Movement

Experience Redesign

Eliminated paper-based money movement entirely — replacing fragmented manual coordination with a single guided digital flow, validated by 10/10 users completing without assistance.

Role

Senior Product Designer

Users

Financial Advisors, Client Services

Scope

User Research, Digital Flows, Paper-to-Digital, Status Visibility, Usability Testing

Status

● Demo Validated

The Challenge

Fragmented complexity across every transfer.

Every money movement request started with paper. Users navigated disconnected approvals, tracked signatures off-system, and re-learned process variations for each transaction type — with no visibility into where their request stood or why it was blocked.

No Digital Trail

Paper forms with no digital trail or audit history.

Chasing Signatures

Signatures chased via email and phone — zero system support.

Opaque Blockers

Users blocked with no explanation or next-step guidance.

Inconsistent Steps

Different steps for every transaction type — re-learned each time.

What I Did

Strategic execution from research to validation.

1

User Research

Discovered users weren't confused by forms — they were confused by what happened after submission. Shadow sessions and interviews revealed the real problem: zero post-submission visibility.

2

Flow Architecture

Collapsed variable multi-step sequences into a single typed flow — one path regardless of transaction type. Removed the cognitive load of re-learning each transfer variant.

3

Status Visibility

Designed progressive status states tied to backend events — users never had to ask "where is my request". Every blocker surfaced an action, not a dead end.

4

Usability Testing

10/10 users completed without assistance. Zero prompted. All 10 reported higher confidence vs the previous process — and zero off-system coordination was needed to complete.

Prototype
